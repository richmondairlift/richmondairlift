Who We Are & What We’re Doing

Richmond Airlift is a small, community-driven effort to help our neighbors during a time of widespread uncertainty. You all know what we’re talking about.

For many people, leaving the house right now isn’t overwhelming — it’s unsafe and scary. Our goal is to make it possible for neighbors to stay in their homes while still getting essential things done.

We intentionally keep groups small. If a group grows too large, this system is designed so new groups can be formed easily. Decentralization isn’t a buzzword here — it’s how we reduce risk, avoid bottlenecks, and make sure no single person or group becomes a point of failure. Helpers are encouraged to spin up new groups as needed.

In this project, neighbors are people requesting help. Helpers are people volunteering their time and effort to provide that help. We’re doing this because our neighbors need support — not because we’re happy about the circumstances that make this necessary.

What Helpers Are Signing Up For

Helpers communicate only through Signal. Signal is an end-to-end encrypted messaging app that minimizes data collection and allows people to participate without exposing personal information. This helps protect both helpers and neighbors.

The structure is intentionally flat. There is no central authority managing every interaction. With that comes responsibility: helpers should be thoughtful, cautious, and intentional about who they add. Anyone added as a helper should be beyond reproach. Our neighbors’ safety is the top priority.

Helper Usernames

To keep conversations clear and to protect neighbors, helpers use a consistent Signal username format.

Helper usernames should be:

  • the + a name of your choosing
  • You may use your real name or a chosen name
  • Examples: theAlex, theRiver, theJordan

This makes it easy for neighbors to identify helpers in the group without exposing phone numbers or other personal details.

In Signal, you can set this by going to Settings → Profile → Username. Be sure to generate and share your username link from inside the Signal app if needed.

How the Signal Group Works

  1. A neighbor posts a request to the Signal group using a shared template (name, task, general area, timing, etc.). No identifying information is included at this stage.
  2. A helper indicates they are taking the task by reacting to the message with an emoji.
  3. The helper then starts a direct message with the neighbor to work out details privately.
  4. Only in the private message are specific details shared, such as an address or grocery list.
  5. The helper completes the task, allowing the neighbor to stay safely at home.
