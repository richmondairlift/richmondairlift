Who We Are

Richmond Airlift is a small, community-based effort to help our neighbors during a time of widespread uncertainty. You know what we are talking about.

For many people, leaving the house right now feels unsafe and scary. We help neighbors stay in their homes while still getting everyday things done. This includes grocery and supply pickups, prescriptions, walking dogs, shoveling snow, mowing grass, and other basic household needs.

We keep groups intentionally small. When a group becomes too large, new groups can be formed so coordination stays simple and safer for everyone involved. If you think a new group is needed, contact an admin to talk through how that can happen.

Neighbors are people requesting help. Helpers are people offering their time and effort. We are doing this because our neighbors need support and we are in a position to provide it.

Tools

Helpers and neighbors communicate only through Signal.

Signal is an end-to-end encrypted messaging app that limits data collection and reduces exposure of personal information. It is available on iOS, Android, Windows, macOS, and Linux.

We will communicate in English as much as possible. Many neighbors may not speak English or may not speak it comfortably. Helpers are strongly encouraged to become familiar with a translation app so communication remains clear and respectful.

Trust is essential. Only invite helpers who you trust completely and whose household shares the same values around safety, discretion, and respect. This is meant to stay quiet and low-profile. Do not share this widely. Share it only with people you would trust without hesitation.

Usernames

To make helpers easy to identify and to protect neighbors, we use a consistent username format.

For this group, helper usernames must follow this format:

  • the + a name of your choosing
  • You may use your real name or a chosen name
  • Examples: theAlex, theRiver, theJordan

This makes it clear who is a helper and helps prevent someone from presenting themselves as a helper when they should not be. Everyone should remain alert and speak up if something feels wrong.

Setting Up Signal

  1. Download Signal from signal.org/download or your app store.
  2. Create your account using your phone number. You do not need to share this number with others.
  3. Set your username under Settings, then Profile, then Username.
  4. Adjust privacy settings so people see your username and not your phone number.
  5. When sharing your contact link, generate the link inside the Signal app and use that link.

Task Process

  1. A neighbor posts a request using a shared template. No identifying information is included.
  2. A helper reacts with an emoji to show they are taking the task.
  3. The helper starts a direct message with the neighbor.
  4. In the direct message, set disappearing messages to one week using the chat settings in Signal.
  5. Only after that are details like addresses or lists shared.
  6. The helper completes the task so the neighbor can remain safe at home.
