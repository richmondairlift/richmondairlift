The Richmond Airlift is a community-run effort created to provide support during a time of widespread uncertainty.

This site is a starting point for connecting people who need help with everyday tasks to a trusted group of volunteers.

What Is the Signal Group?

The Richmond Airlift Signal group is a private, moderated group where people can request help and coordinate assistance.

We use Signal because it is a reliable, widely available messaging app that prioritizes privacy and security. It allows us to communicate clearly while minimizing unnecessary data sharing.

If you do not already have Signal installed, you can download it here:
https://signal.org/download/

Protecting Your Privacy on Signal

Your privacy and safety matter to us. Signal allows you to participate in group conversations without using your real name and without sharing your phone number with others.

We strongly recommend taking the steps below before messaging the admins or joining the group.

Set a Signal Username

A username lets you communicate on Signal without sharing your phone number. Once enabled, people can contact you using your username instead of your number. These instructions assume you are using a mobile device.

  1. Open Signal
  2. At the top left of the home screen, select your initials and go to Settings
  3. Tap your profile at the top
  4. Select Username
  5. Create a username you are comfortable sharing

Hide Your Phone Number

Signal allows you to control who can see your phone number. We recommend hiding it completely.

  1. Open Signal
  2. Go to Settings → Privacy
  3. Set Phone Number Visibility to Nobody
  4. Set Who can find me by my number to Nobody

Why We Recommend This

These steps help protect your identity by limiting what personal information is visible to others. They reduce the risk of unwanted contact and make it easier to participate safely and comfortably.

You do not need to use your real name on Signal. A first name, nickname, or pseudonym is sufficient.
